# October 2nd in history

- 1835
  - The Texas Revolution began with the Battle of Gonzales, where Texian rebels refused to return a cannon to Mexican forces, sparking armed conflict.
- 1869
  - Mahatma Gandhi, the leader of the Indian independence movement against British rule and a pioneer of nonviolent civil disobedience, was born in Porbandar, India.
- 1919
  - U.S. President Woodrow Wilson suffered a debilitating stroke, leaving him partially paralyzed and incapacitated for the remainder of his presidency.
- 1950
  - The comic strip *Peanuts* by Charles M. Schulz was first published in seven U.S. newspapers, introducing characters like Charlie Brown and Snoopy.
- 1958
  - Guinea gained independence from France, becoming the first French African colony to gain independence.
- 1967
  - Thurgood Marshall was sworn in as the first African American justice of the U.S. Supreme Court.
- 2006
  - WikiLeaks, an online platform for whistleblowers, was officially launched by Julian Assange, providing a platform for classified or otherwise sensitive documents to be released.
- 2018
  - Saudi journalist Jamal Khashoggi was last seen entering the Saudi consulate in Istanbul; his assassination sparked a global outcry and diplomatic crisis.
